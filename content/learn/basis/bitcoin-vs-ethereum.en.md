## Bitcoin vs Ethereum (and why you shouldn't confuse them)

---

Bitcoin and Ethereum are often mentioned in the same sentence. But they're completely different things built for different purposes.

Here's the simplest way to understand them.

---

## Bitcoin — digital gold

Bitcoin was created in 2009 as **digital money** — a peer-to-peer electronic cash system.

Its main purpose:

- **Store of value** — like digital gold. Limited supply (21 million coins).
- **Transfer of value** — send money anywhere, anytime, without banks.
- **Censorship resistance** — nobody can stop your transaction or freeze your coins.

Bitcoin is **simple by design**. It does one thing and does it well.

> Think of Bitcoin as **digital gold** — you buy it, hold it, and transfer it when needed.

### Key facts

- Created: 2009 by Satoshi Nakamoto
- Supply: capped at 21 million
- Consensus: [Proof of Work](/en/glossary/#proof-of-work) (energy-intensive, very secure)
- Transactions: ~7 per second
- Average [block](/en/glossary/#block) time: 10 minutes

---

## Ethereum — digital oil

Ethereum launched in 2015 as a **world computer** — a platform for [decentralized](/en/glossary/#decentralization) applications ([dApps](/en/glossary/#dapp)).

Its main purpose:

- **Smart contracts** — programs that run exactly as programmed, without downtime or fraud.
- **dApps** — decentralized apps ([DeFi](/en/glossary/#defi), [NFTs](/en/glossary/#nft), games, social networks).
- **Programmable money** — [tokens](/en/glossary/#token), loans, trading — all automated.

Ethereum is **complex by design**. It's a platform, not just a currency.

> Think of Ethereum as **digital oil** — it powers the decentralized economy.

### Key facts

- Created: 2015 by Vitalik Buterin
- Supply: uncapped (but has burning mechanism)
- Consensus: [Proof of Stake](/en/glossary/#proof-of-stake) (energy-efficient)
- Transactions: ~15-30 per second
- Average [block](/en/glossary/#block) time: ~12 seconds

---

## The key difference

| | Bitcoin | Ethereum |
|---|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Digital money / store of value | [Decentralized](/en/glossary/#decentralization) world computer |
| **Best analogy** | Gold | Oil |
| **What runs on it** | Just transactions (BTC) | Apps, tokens, [NFTs](/en/glossary/#nft), smart contracts |
| **Programmable** | No (intentionally limited) | Yes (Turing-complete) |
| **Supply** | Fixed (21M) | Variable (burn + issue) |
| **Energy use** | High (PoW) | Low (PoS) |
| **Speed** | Slow but secure | Faster, more flexible |

## Which one to choose?

### Choose Bitcoin if:

- You want a **store of value** — like gold but digital
- You want **maximum security** and decentralization
- You're investing long-term (3+ years)
- You want **simplicity** — buy and hold

### Choose Ethereum if:

- You want to **use crypto** — [DeFi](/en/glossary/#defi), NFTs, apps
- You're interested in **programmable money**
- You want to **earn yield** through [staking](/en/glossary/#staking) or DeFi
- You believe in the **future of decentralized apps**

### Both, if:

- You want **diversification** — they serve different purposes
- You believe both digital gold and the programmable economy have value

> **Don't let people tell you "Bitcoin vs Ethereum" — it's not a competition. They're tools for different jobs. Just like you wouldn't compare a hammer to a chainsaw.**

## FAQ

### Can Ethereum replace Bitcoin?

No. They serve different purposes. Bitcoin is digital gold. Ethereum is a platform. You can't replace one with the other.

### Can I use Bitcoin for smart contracts?

Very limited. Bitcoin has basic scripting, but it's intentionally not Turing-complete for security reasons. For complex smart contracts, you need Ethereum or similar platforms.

### Which one is better for beginners?

Bitcoin is simpler to understand and safer for long-term holding. Ethereum is better if you want to explore what crypto can actually do beyond just buying and holding.

### Are they competitors?

Not really. They're different layers of the crypto ecosystem. Many people hold both for different reasons.
