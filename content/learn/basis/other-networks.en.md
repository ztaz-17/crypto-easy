## Other Networks and Coins: Overview of Top Cryptocurrencies

In the world of crypto, Bitcoin and Ether are just the tip of the iceberg. There are dozens of other coins out there, each with its own idea, technology, and audience. Some are built for fast transfers, others for anonymity, and still others for games and apps.

Let's take a look at the biggest cryptocurrencies by market cap (data as of May 2026). I've arranged them in descending order so you can see who's on top today.

---

## XRP (Ripple) — for Bank Transfers

XRP isn't just a [cryptocurrency](/en/glossary/#cryptocurrency) — it's a whole platform for international bank transfers. A regular SWIFT transfer takes 3–5 days; XRP takes seconds with near-zero fees.

**Main selling point:** RippleNet — a network where banks use XRP as a [bridge](/en/glossary/#bridge) currency for settlements.

**Key events in 2025–2026:**
- The SEC finally closed its years-long case — XRP was officially ruled not a security. This triggered a sharp rally and the return of US institutional investors.
- Ripple launched its own [stablecoin](/en/glossary/#stablecoin) RLUSD on XRP Ledger — issuance exceeded $100 million in the first month.
- Major US banks began integrating Ripple for cross-border payments.

> XRP isn't about "making money from volatility." It's a technology for the banking system, and its success is heavily tied to regulators.

---

## Binance Coin (BNB) — the Coin of the Largest Exchange

BNB is the native [token](/en/glossary/#token) of Binance, the world's largest crypto exchange. Initially it gave you a discount on trading fees, but over time it grew into a full ecosystem: BNB is used on the BNB Chain network, for gas fees, [staking](/en/glossary/#staking), and Launchpad participation.

**Main selling point:** BNB Chain is one of the most popular networks for [DeFi](/en/glossary/#defi) and [NFTs](/en/glossary/#nft) — cheap and fast. Plus, Binance burns a portion of tokens every quarter, reducing supply.

**Key events in 2025–2026:**
- The BNB Chain v2.2 upgrade ("Prague" hard [fork](/en/glossary/#fork)) cut [block](/en/glossary/#block) time to 1 second and lowered fees by 40%.
- Binance obtained full VASP licenses in the UAE and Turkey.
- The auto-burn mechanism was updated — the formula now accounts for [DEX](/en/glossary/#dex) and [CEX](/en/glossary/#cex) volume, removing the human factor.

> BNB is a bet on Binance as a company and on BNB Chain as an ecosystem.

## Solana (SOL) — the Fastest L1

Solana is a [blockchain](/en/glossary/#blockchain) designed from the ground up for speed. It handles tens of thousands of transactions per second with pennies in fees. That makes it an ideal environment for DeFi exchanges, games, and mass-market applications.

**Main selling point:** Firedancer — a new validator client that pushed throughput to 50,000 TPS.

**Key events in 2025–2026:**
- Firedancer hit mainnet beta — this is the second independent client for the network (like Geth and Nethermind for Ethereum), making Solana more resilient.
- Solana has outranked Ethereum in DEX volume for three months straight — $120 billion per month.
- The DePIN ([decentralized](/en/glossary/#decentralization) physical infrastructure networks) sector is booming on Solana: Helium, Hivemapper, Render Network.

> Solana sacrifices decentralization for speed. It's a trade-off that millions of users are fine with.

## TRON (TRX) — Stablecoins and Entertainment

TRON is a blockchain that bet big on stablecoins and entertainment apps (gaming, casinos, streaming). It doesn't claim to be "the most decentralized," but when it comes to USDT transaction volume, it's number one in the world.

**Main selling point:** More than 56% of all USDT transfers worldwide go through the TRON network.

**Key events in 2025–2026:**
- USDT supply on TRON hit a record $65 billion.
- TRON integrated Chainlink CCIP — now USDT (TRC-20) can be transferred directly to Ethereum, BNB Chain, and Arbitrum without wrapping.
- BTFS (BitTorrent File System) surpassed 100 million monthly active users.

> If you need a cheap USDT transfer — TRON is your pick. For DeFi and complex smart contracts — look at Ethereum or Solana instead.

## Dogecoin (DOGE) — the First Meme Coin

Dogecoin started as a joke back in 2013 — a cryptocurrency with a dog from a meme. But the community and Elon Musk's support turned it into one of the most recognizable brands in crypto.

**Main selling point:** A huge community, plus adoption as a payment method (Tesla, Dallas Mavericks, X Payments).

**Key events in 2025–2026:**
- X (Twitter) officially integrated Dogecoin for tipping and in-app purchases in the US.
- The Dogecoin Foundation released "Dogebox" — one-click node software for merchants.
- The community voted to lower [inflation](/en/glossary/#inflation) from ~4% to 2.5% per year.

> Dogecoin is still mined (Proof-of-Work), just like Bitcoin. Its inflation is fixed — 5 billion coins per year — which reduces the incentive to hold it speculatively.

## Hyperliquid (HYPE) — Decentralized Exchange

Hyperliquid is a blockchain built specifically for decentralized derivatives trading. It runs on its own HyperBFT consensus, delivering centralized-exchange speed.

**Main selling point:** The first DEX to process $1 trillion in cumulative trading volume.

**Key events in 2025–2026:**
- Hyperliquid crossed the $1 trillion cumulative trading volume mark, becoming the largest derivatives DEX.
- HYPE staking went live with ~25% APY — $500 million was staked in the first week.
- HyperEVM — an Ethereum-compatible layer for smart contracts — lets you run ERC-20 tokens and DeFi protocols directly on Hyperliquid.

> Hyperliquid is a bet that the future of trading belongs to decentralized exchanges, and that speed can catch up to CEXs.

## LEO Token (UNUS SED LEO) — Bitfinex's Token

LEO is the ecosystem token of Bitfinex and its related projects (Tether, Bitfinex Securities, Capital Markets). It gives you trading fee discounts and is burned using platform revenue.

**Main selling point:** LEO is practically non-volatile — in 2025–2026 it traded in a tight $8–10.5 range, because its price is supported by Bitfinex's buyback program.

**Key events in 2025–2026:**
- Bitfinex reached 30 million users; the LEO buyback was increased to $20 million per month.
- LEO was integrated into Bitfinex Securities — tokenized US Treasuries and stocks.
- Bitfinex Capital Markets revenues were added to the LEO buyback pool.

> LEO is a utility token for active Bitfinex users. As an investment, it's closer to a corporate bond than a cryptocurrency.

## Bitcoin Cash (BCH) — Bitcoin for Payments

Bitcoin Cash split off from Bitcoin in 2017 over a scaling dispute. The core idea: bigger blocks = more transactions = a payment system for everyday purchases.

**Main selling point:** Block size was increased to 64 MB (after the 2026 upgrade) — up to 15,000 TPS.

**Key events in 2025–2026:**
- The "BlackRock" hard fork: 64 MB blocks + Schnorr multisignatures for privacy.
- BitPay started accepting BCH for settlements by default — 500,000 payments in Q4 2025 alone.
- Atomic swaps with Lightning Network — BCH can be "bridged" to LN for instant micropayments.

> If Bitcoin is digital gold, then Bitcoin Cash is digital cash for daily spending.

## Cardano (ADA) — a Scientific Approach to Blockchain

Cardano is a blockchain built on peer-reviewed academic research. It's one of the few that has used Proof-of-Stake from day one (since 2017).

**Main selling point:** Ouroboros Leios — a new consensus mechanism that pushed TPS past 1,000+.

**Key events in 2025–2026:**
- Ouroboros Leios went live on mainnet — parallel block creation while maintaining full security.
- The largest RWA project on a blockchain — the Ethiopian government launched a land registry pilot on Cardano (2 million plots).
- PartnerChain SDK — a tool for launching private consortium sidechains.

> Cardano develops slowly but thoroughly. If a scientific foundation and long-term sustainability matter to you — this is your pick.

## Monero (XMR) — the King of Privacy

Monero is the most popular private cryptocurrency. Unlike Bitcoin, where every transaction is visible on the explorer, Monero hides the sender, receiver, and amount.

**Main selling point:** Every transaction is a mix of several fake fingerprints (ring signatures) plus a hidden amount (RingCT).

**Key events in 2025–2026:**
- The Seraphis / Full-Chain Membership Proofs (FCMP) upgrade — Monero is now resistant to quantum attacks.
- OKX Japan re-listed Monero after Japan updated its privacy coin regulations.
- Monero captured 92% of all darknet market transactions, cementing its position as the go-to payment tool for confidential settlements.

> Monero is the only truly anonymous cryptocurrency in the top 100. That's why exchanges periodically delist it — regulators don't like it.

## Sui (SUI) — a New L1 on Move

Sui is a next-generation blockchain built on the Move language (originally developed at Facebook for Libra). It stands out for its speed and parallel transaction processing.

**Main selling point:** Transaction finality in 0.39 ms — faster than any other L1.

**Key events in 2025–2026:**
- Mysticeti consensus — 390 ms to finality, making Sui officially the fastest L1.
- BlackRock launched a BUIDL fund on Sui — $200 million [TVL](/en/glossary/#tvl) in the first month.
- MoveGPT — the first AI agent framework on Sui, letting you deploy trading bots and NFT minters directly on-chain.

> Sui is a dark horse. Lower market cap, but one of the most advanced technologies out there. Part of the Move ecosystem (Aptos, Sui).

---

## Avalanche (AVAX) — a Network for Corporate Subnets

Avalanche is a blockchain platform focused on launching custom networks (subnets). It's one of the leaders in real-world asset tokenization (RWA).

**Main selling point:** Subnets — isolated blockchains that are still secured by the main network.

**Key events in 2025–2026:**
- Avalanche9000: subnet launch cost dropped from 2,000 AVAX to 20 AVAX. Proof-of-Stake Light — a subnet for $10/month.
- State Street launched a tokenized money market fund on Avalanche worth $500 million — the largest RWA fund on any L1.
- AWS launched a managed "Cloud Subnets" service — deploy a corporate subnet in one click.

> If you're interested in asset tokenization and enterprise blockchains — Avalanche is one of the leading platforms in this space.

---

## Toncoin (TON) — Crypto Inside Telegram

Toncoin is a blockchain created by the Telegram team (originally Telegram Open Network). After Telegram stepped away from the project, the community took over, and in 2024–2026 Telegram got back into integration.

**Main selling point:** TON is the only blockchain built into a messaging app with 900 million users.

**Key events in 2025–2026:**
- 100 million active [wallets](/en/glossary/#wallet) per month — a record growth rate.
- Telegram "Stars" (in-app currency) migrated to the TON blockchain — 900 million users got an on-chain experience.
- TON Society + DWF Labs launched a $500 million fund for DeFi, games, and AI agents on TON.

> TON is crypto for the masses: a wallet inside Telegram with a ready-made audience. If you want to try cryptocurrencies without installing third-party apps — start with TON.

## Which One Should You Pick?

**If you're a beginner:** start with Solana (cheap and fast) or TON (a wallet inside Telegram).

**If you need stablecoins for transfers:** TRON is the de facto standard for USDT.

**If you're interested in investments:** look into projects with real products and yield — Hyperliquid (HYPE), Sui (SUI), Avalanche (AVAX).

**If privacy matters:** Monero is the only real choice, but keep in mind it's harder to buy on exchanges.

**If the "corporate" side of crypto appeals to you:** XRP (banking) or Cardano (government projects).

**If you want a community-driven story:** Dogecoin — a surprisingly resilient joke.

> I recommend reading about other coins from the "Basics" section too: Bitcoin and Ether are the foundation of all crypto, and stablecoins are the most popular category in the world.
