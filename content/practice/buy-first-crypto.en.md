## How to Buy Your First Crypto

---

You've created a wallet. Now the logical question: **how do you put crypto in it?**

You can buy on an exchange. Directly from another person. Through an instant swap service. Or not buy at all — we'll cover that too.

---

## Method 1: Crypto Exchange

The most popular and convenient way. Register on an exchange, fund your account, and buy Bitcoin, USDT, or any coin.

### Popular exchanges for beginners

| Exchange | Best for |
|----------|----------|
| **Coinbase** | Easiest start — buy directly with card, licensed in US and EU |
| **Binance** | Largest, lowest fees |
| **Bybit** | Clean interface, popular in Europe and Asia |
| **KuCoin** | Many altcoins, no KYC for small amounts |
| **OKX** | Good P2P marketplace |

### Pros ✅
- Huge selection of coins
- High liquidity
- P2P markets — buy directly from other people
- Direct card purchase (Visa, Mastercard)

### Cons ❌
- KYC (identity verification) needed for full access
- Exchange holds your coins — it's custodial
- If the exchange freezes your account, you lose access

### Quick steps:
1. Register on Coinbase, Binance, or Bybit
2. Fund your account (card, P2P, or transfer)
3. Buy your coin (USDT, BTC, ETH)
4. **Crucial!** Withdraw to your own wallet (MetaMask, Trust Wallet)

> ❗️ Don't leave money on exchanges longer than needed. Exchanges get hacked, frozen, shut down. Buy → withdraw.

---

## What is KYC?

KYC (Know Your Customer) is identity verification that exchanges are legally required to perform. Usually involves uploading a photo of your ID or passport and a selfie.

### Do you need it?
- **Under $500-1000**: usually optional, limited but functional
- **Above that**: required for higher limits and features

> Tip: try without KYC first. If limits aren't enough — verify.

---

## Method 2: P2P (Peer-to-Peer)

Buy crypto directly from another person. The exchange acts as escrow.

### How P2P works:
1. Find a seller (filter by price, payment method, limits)
2. Send them money (card, bank transfer)
3. Seller confirms receipt — exchange releases crypto to you
4. If seller doesn't deliver — exchange refunds you

### Pros ✅
- No KYC needed on many platforms
- Pay with local currency directly from your card
- Protected by exchange dispute system

### Cons ❌
- Slightly higher rates than spot market
- Risk of bank card freeze if seller used dirty money
- Must check seller rating carefully

### Choosing a P2P seller:
- **100+ completed trades**, 98-100% rating
- Registration date — not yesterday
- Verified / Trusted badge

---

## Method 3: Instant Swap Services

Services like **Mercuryo, MoonPay, Guardarian** — you pay by card, crypto arrives in your wallet within minutes.

### Best for:
- Quick small purchases ($10-500)
- No registration hassles
- Directly inside MetaMask / Trust Wallet ("Buy" button)

### Downside:
- Fees 2-5% (higher than exchange)
- Limited coin selection
- KYC for larger amounts

---

## Method 4: Bitcoin ATMs

Physical terminals where you insert cash and get BTC. **High fees (10-15%), limited locations. Not recommended for first purchase.**

## What should a beginner choose?

**You have a bank card, okay with verification:**
1. Register on Coinbase or Binance
2. Pass KYC
3. Buy USDT or BTC with card
4. Withdraw to your wallet
→ 10-15 minutes total.

**Don't want to show ID:**
→ P2P on Bybit or KuCoin without KYC, or buy through MetaMask's built-in swap.

**Need it quick and small:**
→ Open MetaMask → "Buy" → Mercuryo/MoonPay → pay by card.

---

## Risks to know

| Risk | Prevention |
|------|-----------|
| P2P scammers | Never pay outside the platform interface |
| Fake swap sites | Only use swap services built into your wallet |
| Bank card freeze | Buy from sellers with 100+ trades |
| Keeping coins on exchange | Withdraw to your own wallet immediately |

---

## Can you get crypto for free?

Yes — **airdrops**. Projects give away tokens for simple actions (following, registering). But you need a wallet and a little gas. Start with $10-20 USDT first.

---

## Conclusion

Buying your first crypto is easier than it seems.

| Situation | Best method |
|-----------|------------|
| First time, have card | Coinbase / Binance |
| No ID | P2P or wallet swap |
| Small and fast | Mercuryo/MoonPay in MetaMask |
| Anonymous | Bitcoin ATM (if available) |

> **Golden rule: don't store crypto on an exchange. Buy → withdraw to your wallet.**

## FAQ

### How much for a first purchase?
$10-20. Enough to test everything.

### Which crypto to buy first?
**USDT** — stablecoin, pegged to $1. Or **Bitcoin** for the classic.

### Can I buy with a card?
Yes. Coinbase, Binance, and swap services (Mercuryo, MoonPay) take Visa/Mastercard.

### What after buying?
Withdraw to your non-custodial wallet, write down your seed phrase, keep it safe.
