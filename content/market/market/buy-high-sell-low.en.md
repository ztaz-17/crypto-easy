## Why You Buy at the Top and Sell at the Bottom

---

Sound familiar?

You see a coin pump 80% in a day. Every chat is screaming "WAGMI", "to the moon", "buy now!" You can't resist — you buy at the very peak.

Two days later — you're down 40%.

You wait. It drops more. Fear grips you. In a panic, you sell, locking in the loss.

And the coin... reverses and shoots up. Without you.

---

> **Buying at the top and selling at the bottom** — it's not a fluke. It's a pattern.

In this article, we'll break down:

>- why your brain makes you act against your own best interests;
>- how **FOMO** and panic selling actually work;
>- and how to break the cycle for good.

---

## The Psychology: Why Your Brain Works Against You

### Evolution Didn't Prepare Us for the Market

Our brain was shaped over thousands of years in an environment where survival was everything:

|- **danger → flee**;
|- **scarce resource → grab it**;
|- **the herd is moving → move with the herd**.

On the savanna, that kept us alive. In crypto, it destroys your portfolio.

The problem is that the market is designed **against our intuition**:

| Intuition                 | What happens on the market              |
|---------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Everyone's buying → jump in | You buy at the peak                   |
| Everything's dropping → run  | You sell at the bottom                |
| Hurry! Now! Quickly!         | You make an impulsive decision        |

👉 **The market punishes herd behavior.**

---

### The Two Big Traps

In crypto trading, there are two emotional states that reliably wipe out a beginner's deposit.

**The first is FOMO.**  
**The second is panic.**

And they work as a pair.

---

## FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out)

### What It Looks Like

[FOMO](/en/glossary/#fomo) is the fear that you're missing your shot at getting rich.

Symptoms:

- you're constantly checking charts and chats;
- you see a green candle and feel anxious;
- you think "the train is about to leave the station";
- you enter a trade without a plan — just to get in on time.

It works exactly one time out of ten. The other nine, you buy at the top.

---

### Why FOMO Hits So Hard in Crypto

Crypto is the perfect breeding ground for FOMO:

- prices swing **50–200%** in days;
- social media is noise, memes, and "to the moon" screaming;
- it feels like everyone around you is already rich, and you're the fool.

But here's the truth:

> **when everyone is shouting "buy" — the smart money is usually already selling.**

Whales and market makers use the crowd's FOMO to **unload coins at the highest price**. You're buying from them.

---

### Example

Coin X was $1. It ran up to $5 in a week. The chats are euphoric. You buy at $5.

Next day — $4.50. You hold. A week later — $2.

You're in the red, while the people who bought at $1 have already taken profits.

---

## Panic: Selling at the Bottom

### When Fear Overrides Reason

Panic is the flip side of FOMO.

Once you've bought in and the price starts dropping, an ancient mechanism kicks in: **"danger — run."**

Symptoms:

- red candles trigger physical anxiety;
- you can't sleep, checking the chart every 10 minutes;
- you're convinced "it's all over" and the coin will go to zero;
- you sell, just to make the pain stop.

And it kicks in exactly when the market is about to turn around.

---

### Why Panic Makes You Sell at a Loss

Pull up any 5-year Bitcoin chart.

Sure, there were 50%, 70%, even 90% drawdowns — but every single time the price recovered and hit new highs.

But in the middle of a panic, it always feels like **"this time it's different."**

> **Selling at the bottom is not a strategy. It's an emotion disguised as a decision.**

---

### Example

Let's continue the Coin X story.

You bought at $5. The coin dropped to $2.

Fear locks you up. You stare at the chart — all red. You read the chats — everyone's panicking.

You sell at $1.80. You lock in a 64% loss.

A month later, the coin is worth $8.

---

## Emotion → Action → Result

| Emotion  | Action                   | Result                         |
|----------|--------------------------|--------------------------------|
| FOMO     | Buy at the peak          | You buy expensive              |
| Hope     | Hold a falling asset     | Loss grows                     |
| Panic    | Sell at the bottom       | You lock in the loss           |
| Regret   | Watch it pump without you| FOMO all over again            |
| Greed    | Leverage, margin         | Margin call, lose everything   |

👉 **A closed loop.** Emotions lead to losses. Losses amplify the emotions.

## How to Stop Buying High and Selling Low

### Step 1. Admit: You're Not a Trader

Most people can't trade. And that's completely fine.

Trading is a profession. Like surgery or flying a plane. You wouldn't try to land a 747 after a YouTube course, would you?

> **Admitting you're not a trader is the first step to stop losing money.**

If you're a beginner — don't trade. Just buy and hold.

---

### Step 2. Remove Emotion From the Process

Emotions are the enemy of rational decisions.

What to do:

- **stop checking the chart every hour** — check once a day or once a week;
- **don't read chat groups during a panic** — you'll only find panic there;
- **accept volatility** — 30–50% drops in crypto are normal, not a disaster.

---

### Step 3. Use DCA (Dollar Cost Averaging)

**DCA (Dollar Cost Averaging)** is a strategy where you buy an asset in regular fixed amounts, regardless of price.

How it works:

- instead of buying $1000 all at once;
- you buy $100 every week for 10 weeks.

Benefits:

- you stop guessing where the bottom is;
- you buy during both dips and rallies;
- your average entry price gets smoothed out.

👉 **DCA is the best strategy for anyone who doesn't want to worry about market timing.**

---

### Step 4. Make a Plan and Stick to It

Before you enter a trade, write down:

- **entry target** — at what price and why?;
- **exit target** — at what price do you take profit?;
- **stop-loss** — at what level do you get out if things go wrong.

And the most important rule:

> **follow the plan, not your emotions.**

If you planned to sell at +50% — sell. Even if it feels like it could go higher.

If your stop-loss triggered — don't jump back into the same coin on emotions.

---

### Step 5. Stop Chasing "100x"

Many beginners think: "I'll buy a coin for $100 and a month later it'll be $10,000."

It happens. But usually — it doesn't.

**Chasing 100x is the fastest way to lose everything.**

A much safer approach:

- buy top coins (Bitcoin, Ethereum);
- use DCA;
- hold long-term (at least 1–3 years).

---

## The Real Secret

> **The people who make money in crypto aren't the ones who know how to buy at the top. They're the ones who know how not to sell at the bottom.**

The most successful crypto investors:

- bought Bitcoin at $200;
- held when it dropped to $100;
- held when it dropped to $3,000 in 2020;
- and sold at $60,000+.

They weren't afraid of corrections. They understood that **a dip is a discount, not a disaster.**

---

## Conclusion

Buying at the top and selling at the bottom isn't stupidity. It's a **psychological trap** almost every beginner falls into.

The good news: you can escape it.

All you need to do:

>- admit that emotions are driving your decisions;
>- stop trading and start investing;
>- use **DCA** instead of trying to catch the bottom;
>- make a plan and follow it;
>- and most importantly — **don't give in to the herd instinct**.

## FAQ

### Why do I buy at the peak even though I know it's a mistake?
Because emotions (FOMO) override rational thinking. Your brain is afraid of missing an "opportunity" and acts impulsively.

### How do I stop panic selling?
Stop looking at the chart every 5 minutes. Set a plan (stop-loss and take-profit) and walk away. Volatility is normal.

### Does DCA really work?
Yes. DCA (Dollar Cost Averaging) is statistically one of the most reliable strategies for long-term investors. It removes the need to time the market.

### What if I already bought at the top?
Don't panic sell. If the asset is fundamentally strong — wait for recovery or average down with DCA. Selling at the bottom is the worst thing you can do.

### Will a trading bot help me?
No. Without market understanding and discipline, a bot will just lose your money faster. Work on your psychology first.
