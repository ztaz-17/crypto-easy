## TVL: How to Tell How Much Money Trusts a Protocol

---

You found a new [DeFi](/en/glossary/#defi) protocol. Fancy website, low fees, promising 500% APY.

One question pops up:

> **Is it safe to put money in?**

One of the main tools to answer that is **[TVL](/en/glossary/#tvl)** (Total Value Locked).

## What Is TVL

**TVL** (Total Value Locked) is the total value of all assets locked in a protocol's smart contracts.

In simple terms:

> **how much money users have trusted this protocol with.**

TVL includes:
- [tokens](/en/glossary/#token) in [liquidity](/en/glossary/#liquidity) pools (like on Uniswap);
- deposits in lending protocols (like Aave);
- collateral in [stablecoin](/en/glossary/#stablecoin) protocols (like MakerDAO);
- [staking](/en/glossary/#staking) in liquid staking protocols (like Lido).

The higher the TVL — the more liquidity in the protocol, the harder it is to manipulate.

---

## Why Look at TVL

### 1. Market Trust

High TVL is a signal:

> **"Millions of dollars trust this protocol."**

If a protocol has $10 billion locked — that's no accident. Thousands of users did their own research and decided it was safe.

A TVL of $10K doesn't necessarily mean it's a scam. But it's a reason to ask questions: why are so few people willing to trust this project with their money?

### 2. Liquidity

Protocols with low TVL are vulnerable:

- pools may not have enough funds for swaps;
- one large withdrawal can crash the protocol;
- price manipulation takes less capital.

### 3. Real Activity

TVL shows facts, not promises:

> **real people put in real money.**

Marketing claims can be loud. TVL lies less often.

## How to Read TVL: Three Scenarios

### Scenario 1: TVL Is Growing Steadily

The protocol is attracting new capital. Users trust it. The product works.

> **Good signal.** But not a reason to invest blindly — check other metrics too.

### Scenario 2: TVL Crashed

TVL dropped 50–90% in a short period.

Possible reasons:

- **vulnerability or hack** — users withdrew in panic;
- **yield decline** — farmers moved to another protocol;
- **regulatory risk** — bans or restrictions in key jurisdictions;
- **changing market conditions** — lending rates dropped, stablecoins lost their peg.

> **A sharp TVL drop is a red flag.** If 90% of funds left a protocol — that's no coincidence.

### Scenario 3: TVL Is High but Falling Fast

TVL = $1B. Yesterday it was $5B. Today — $3B.

Here **the trend** matters. One day of decline could be a correction. A week of decline — a systemic problem.

On DefiLlama, look at the 30–90 day chart. If the line is steadily going down — don't ignore it.

## Why a Sharp TVL Drop Is a Red Flag

### "Funds Leaving" = Lost Trust

When TVL drops 90%, it means:

> **90% of users decided to take their money out.**

People don't pull funds from a reliable protocol for no reason.

### Historical Examples

Hacks that wiped out TVL:

- **Poly Network** (2021) — $611M stolen, TVL dropped to zero;
- **Wormhole** (2022) — $326M, TVL collapsed;
- **Mango Markets** (2022) — $114M, TVL sank 80%.

In every case, the TVL drop was the first visible sign of trouble.

Even without a hack — a sharp TVL drop often precedes a protocol shutting down.

## What TVL Doesn't Show

TVL is an important metric, but not the only one.

**TVL does not show:**

- code security (audits, bugs);
- [decentralization](/en/glossary/#decentralization) (who controls the protocol);
- yield (APY can be high while TVL is low);
- real user count (10 [wallets](/en/glossary/#wallet) with $1M each isn't mass adoption);
- custodial risks (some protocols inflate TVL with their own deposits).

> **TVL is an indicator, not a guarantee.**

## How to Use TVL in Practice

A simple checklist before investing:

1. **Open DefiLlama** — find the protocol.
2. **Check current TVL** — over $10M? That's a start.
3. **Look at the 90-day chart** — line going down? Stop.
4. **Compare with competitors** — why this protocol instead of the market leader with 10x the TVL?
5. **Check TVL distribution** — is 80% of the capital sitting in one pool?

If the protocol passes all checks — TVL isn't lying. If not — dig deeper.

## FAQ

### What is TVL in simple terms?

TVL (Total Value Locked) is the total value of all assets users have locked in a DeFi protocol. The higher the TVL, the more trust the protocol has.

### What TVL is considered safe?

There's no exact threshold. Serious protocols typically have $100M+ in TVL. But rather than the absolute number, look at the trend: steady growth or a plateau is good, a sharp drop is bad.

### Where to check TVL?

Use **[DefiLlama](https://defillama.com)** — the most complete and independent TVL aggregator. For deeper analysis — **Dune Analytics**.

### Can TVL be faked?

Yes. Some protocols inflate their TVL by depositing their own funds or using "empty" pools with multiple counting. That's why it's important to verify TVL through independent sources (DefiLlama) and look at the distribution of funds.

### What should I do if a protocol's TVL dropped 90%?

Don't invest. Withdraw your funds if they're already in the protocol. A 90% TVL drop is almost always a sign of a serious problem: a hack, lost trust, or an imminent shutdown.
