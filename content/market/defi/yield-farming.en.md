## Yield Farming: How to Earn in DeFi

---

You've probably seen the ads: "1000% APY!", "Earn crypto with zero effort!", "[DeFi](/en/glossary/#defi) is the new oil!"

Sounds too good to be true. But behind those numbers there's actually a working mechanism — **Yield Farming**. And yes, some people really do make money.

But here's the catch: the higher the yield, the higher the risk.

In this article we'll cover:

>- what yield farming is and how it works;
>- where those crazy interest rates come from;
>- what risks you need to know before putting in your first dollar.

---

## What Is Yield Farming

**Yield Farming** (also called **[liquidity](/en/glossary/#liquidity) [mining](/en/glossary/#mining)**) means lending your crypto assets to liquidity pools on [decentralized](/en/glossary/#decentralization) protocols (DEXs, lending platforms) in exchange for rewards.

In simple terms:

> you give your tokens to a protocol as "rent," and it pays you interest.

Returns are measured in **APY** (Annual Percentage Yield) — the yearly interest rate with compounding factored in. The higher the APY, the more you can potentially earn.

---

## How It Works

### 1. You pick a protocol

For example, **Uniswap**, **Curve**, **Aave**, **Compound**, or **PancakeSwap**. Each offers pools for different [token](/en/glossary/#token) pairs.

### 2. You deposit liquidity

Say there's an ETH/USDT pool. You need to deposit **an equal value of both tokens** (50% ETH, 50% USDT). In return you get **LP tokens** ([Liquidity](/en/glossary/#liquidity) Provider tokens) — your receipt for a share of the pool.

### 3. The protocol pays you

Your earnings come from two sources:

- **protocol fees** — every swap in the pool charges a fee (usually 0.1–0.3%), which gets split among all liquidity providers;
- **governance tokens** — many protocols also hand out their native tokens (CRV, UNI, CAKE) as a bonus.

### 4. You claim your profit

LP tokens can be redeemed to get back your original assets plus the fees you've earned.

---

## Where Does 1000% APY Come From?

This is where it gets interesting. That kind of yield **doesn't come out of thin air**.

It's made up of three components:

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Pool fees | Real trading fees collected from traders |
| Reward tokens | The protocol prints its own tokens and gives them to liquidity providers |
| [Inflation](/en/glossary/#inflation) | The more tokens are emitted, the higher the APY (but the token price may drop) |

> **Important:** High APY is often driven by aggressive token emissions. If the token's price drops faster than you earn, you're still in the red.

## Types of Yield Farming

### Classic (Liquidity Mining)

You deposit a token pair into a [DEX](/en/glossary/#dex) pool (Uniswap, PancakeSwap) and earn fees plus protocol tokens.

### Lending

You lend assets through Aave or Compound and earn interest. Less risky, but the returns are lower.

### Leveraged Farming

You take out a loan inside a protocol and deposit it into another pool. This multiplies both profit and risk. **Not for beginners.**

### Auto-Farming (Yield Aggregators)

Services like Yearn Finance automatically hunt for the best yields and move your funds around. You just deposit — they handle the rest.

## Risks of Yield Farming

### 1. Impermanent Loss

This is the most common and sneaky risk.

When the price of one token in a pool changes relative to the other, you lose part of your capital compared to just holding.

> Example: you deposit 1 ETH ($2000) + 2000 USDT. ETH rises to $3000. If you'd just held the ETH, you'd have $5000. But if you withdraw from the pool, you'll have ~$4800. That $200 difference is **impermanent loss**.

Why is it "impermanent"? Because if the price goes back, the loss disappears. But if you withdraw while the price is still off, the loss becomes real.

**When IL is especially dangerous:**

- high volatility;
- a pool pairing a [stablecoin](/en/glossary/#stablecoin) with a volatile asset;
- holding the position for a long time.

### 2. Rug Pull

The developers set up a pool, lure users in with sky-high yields, then pull the liquidity — and it's gone. No more money.

**How to protect yourself:**

- check the smart contract code;
- see if there's an audit;
- don't jump into brand-new projects promising 10,000% APY.

### 3. Smart Contract Bugs

Even audited protocols can have bugs. Examples: the Nomad [Bridge](/en/glossary/#bridge) hack ($190M), incidents with Cream Finance.

### 4. Token Price Drops

You get paid in the protocol's native tokens. If their price crashes 90%, your real return could be negative.

### 5. High Gas Fees (on Ethereum)

On Ethereum every operation (deposit, withdraw, reinvest) costs gas. With active farming, fees can eat up all your profit.

## Strategies for Beginners

Start with the simplest and safest options:

1. **Lending stablecoins** (USDC/USDT/DAI on Aave or Compound) — a steady 3–8% per year with no impermanent loss.
2. **Stablecoin LP pool** (Curve 3pool) — 5–15% with minimal IL.
3. **Auto-farming** (Yearn Finance) — deposit your funds and everything is automated.

**What NOT to do:**

- enter a pool with a token you don't understand;
- chase APY above 500%;
- take out loans to farm when you're just starting out;
- invest money you can't afford to lose.

## How to Choose a Pool

A checklist before you deposit:

- [ ] Does the protocol have an audit? (from CertiK, Trail of Bits, OpenZeppelin)
- [ ] How long has the project been around?
- [ ] How much liquidity is in the pool? ([TVL](/en/glossary/#tvl) — total value locked)
- [ ] What's the trading volume?
- [ ] Who's on the project team? Is there any info about them?
- [ ] How much of the APY comes from reward tokens vs actual fees?

## Conclusion

Yield Farming is a real way to earn in [DeFi](/en/glossary/#defi). It gives you access to returns you can't get in traditional finance. But it won't turn $100 into $1000 in a month without risk.

> The higher the promised yield, the higher the chance you'll lose your capital.

Successful farming isn't about greed. It's about:

- understanding the mechanics;
- diversifying;
- cold, hard calculation;
- being ready to lose what you put in.

## FAQ

### How much can you earn from yield farming?
It depends on your capital, strategy, and market conditions. Conservative earnings: 5–15% per year in stablecoins. Aggressive strategies: 50% to unlimited (with corresponding risk).

### What is impermanent loss in simple terms?
It's the missed profit when one token in a pool sharply rises (or falls) in price. You earn less than if you'd just held the asset.

### Is yield farming passive income?
Sort of. It requires monitoring your positions, tracking fees, and reinvesting. A true "set and forget" approach only works with aggregators like Yearn Finance.

### What about taxes on yield farming?
In most countries, every operation (deposit, reward claim, withdrawal) is a taxable event. It's recommended to track everything with tools like Koinly, CoinTracker, or Accointing.

### Where should a beginner start?
Open Aave or Compound, deposit USDC at 4–8% APY. Understand the mechanics. Only then move on to DEX pools.
